#+title: Color Theory for Theme Design
#+author: Hossam Saraya
#+property: header-args :tangle no

Lessons from analyzing Everforest, Solarized, Nord, Catppuccin, and Gruvbox —
and how Ember applies them.

* Rule 1: Background Tint = Theme Identity

Every great theme tints the background toward one hue. Pure neutral grey is bland.

| Theme       | BG Hex    | Hue   | Character          |
|-------------+-----------+-------+--------------------|
| Everforest  | =#2d353b= | H180  | green-grey, forest |
| Solarized   | =#002b36= | H192  | dark teal          |
| Nord        | =#2e3440= | H220  | blue-grey, arctic  |
| Catppuccin  | =#1e1e2e= | H240  | purple-blue, warm  |
| Gruvbox     | =#282828= | H30   | warm brown, retro  |
| *Ember*     | =#1c1b19= | H40   | warm graphite      |

*Takeaway:* Pick a hue. Tint everything toward it. The hue IS the theme.

Ember chose warm graphite — a subtle yellow-brown tint (H40) at very low
saturation. The warmth is felt, not seen.

* Rule 2: Foreground is Never Pure White

All ergonomic themes use tinted, softened foreground. Pure white (#ffffff)
causes eye strain on dark backgrounds.

| Theme      | FG Hex    | Lightness | Tint        |
|------------+-----------+-----------+-------------|
| Everforest | =#d3c6aa= | 74%       | warm tan    |
| Solarized  | =#839496= | 58%       | blue-grey   |
| Nord       | =#d8dee9= | 87%       | cool blue   |
| Catppuccin | =#cdd6f4= | 88%       | lavender    |
| Gruvbox    | =#ebdbb2= | 81%       | cream       |
| *Ember*    | =#d8d0c0= | 80%       | warm ivory  |

*Target:* FG lightness 58-88%, tinted toward the theme's hue.
Solarized is notably dim (58%) — this is intentional for low contrast.

Ember's ivory foreground (#d8d0c0) matches the warm graphite background,
creating a cohesive temperature throughout.

* Rule 3: Equal Perceptual Brightness Across Accents

This is the biggest insight from Solarized. All 8 accent colors sit at the
*same CIELAB lightness (~L*50)*. Everforest and Nord achieve this intuitively.

Why this matters:
- No single color screams louder than another
- Your eye doesn't jump to red or yellow first
- Code looks balanced, not like a Christmas tree
- You can scan structure without being pulled to specific tokens

*Target:* All accents at CIELAB L*45-60 for dark themes.

Ember places its 8 muted accents in a tight L*52-58 band. Then coral (#e08060)
sits at S55% — the single saturated hero that breaks the calm. This is
intentional: one ember in the ash.

How to verify: convert each accent to CIELAB and check the L* values.
Online tool: https://colorjs.io/apps/convert/

* Rule 4: Moderate Saturation

| Theme       | Typical Accent Saturation | Strategy              |
|-------------+---------------------------+-----------------------|
| Everforest  | 30-45%                    | Very muted, natural   |
| Solarized   | 60-80%                    | High sat, low L*      |
| Nord        | 25-40%                    | Very muted, pastel    |
| Catppuccin  | 50-70%                    | Pastel (high L + mod S) |
| Gruvbox     | 60-80%                    | High sat, warm-shifted |
| *Ember*     | 20-35%                    | Ultra-muted except coral |

Two strategies for avoiding harshness:
1. *Low saturation* (Everforest, Nord) — colors are muted/dusty
2. *High saturation + low lightness* (Solarized) — vivid but dark
3. *High saturation + high lightness* (Catppuccin) — pastel effect

Avoid: high saturation + high lightness + dark background = neon, eye strain.

Ember takes strategy 1 to an extreme: all accents are ultra-muted (S20-35%),
then a single accent (coral) breaks through at S55%. This "monochrome hero"
approach creates focus without visual fatigue.

*Target:* Saturation under 60%, or compensate with adjusted lightness.

* Rule 5: Smooth Background Ramp (8-13 steps)

All themes define a gradient from darkest to lightest, sharing the same hue.
This ramp is used for UI layering (popups, modeline, hl-line, etc.).

| Theme      | Steps | Range                               |
|------------+-------+-------------------------------------|
| Catppuccin | 13    | Crust -> Mantle -> Base -> Surface -> Overlay -> Subtext -> Text |
| Gruvbox    | 8     | bg -> bg1-4 -> fg4-1 -> fg            |
| Solarized  | 8     | base03 -> base02 -> ... -> base3      |
| Nord       | 7     | nord0-3 (dark) + nord4-6 (light)    |
| Everforest | 8     | bg_dim -> bg -> bg1-5 -> fg           |
| *Ember*    | 11    | base0-8 + fg + fg-alt               |

More steps = smoother layering, fewer visible jumps between UI elements.

*Target:* At least 8 stops, all sharing the theme hue.

* Rule 6: Contrast Ratio (BG to FG)

| Theme      | Approx Ratio | Feel         |
|------------+--------------+--------------|
| Solarized  | ~7:1         | low, relaxed |
| Everforest | ~8:1         | comfortable  |
| *Ember*    | ~8.5:1       | comfortable  |
| Nord       | ~9:1         | moderate     |
| Catppuccin | ~10:1        | moderate     |
| Gruvbox    | ~11:1        | crisp        |

WCAG AA requires 4.5:1 minimum. Most editors aim for 7-11:1.

Ember targets 8.5:1 — comfortable for long coding sessions while meeting
WCAG AA. The Ember Soft variant reduces this slightly; Ember Light maintains
the same ratio with inverted polarity.

*Target:* 7-10:1 for comfortable long-session reading.
Pure black bg (#000000) with bright fg pushes ratio to 12:1+ — harsh.

* Rule 7: Warm vs Cool — Pick One and Commit

- *Warm:* Gruvbox, Everforest (browns, tans, olive greens)
- *Cool:* Nord, Solarized (blues, teals, blue-greys)
- *Mixed warm:* Catppuccin (purple base + pastel accents)
- *All-warm:* Ember (warm graphite bg + warm ivory fg + warm accents)

*Rule:* Don't mix warm and cool randomly. Your accent hue range should
span ~120 degrees of the color wheel, not 360 degrees.

Ember commits fully to warmth. The accent hues span roughly 90 degrees
(olive at H80 through rose at H0), all clustered in the warm quadrant.
Even the coolest accent (steel at H205) has a muted, warm character.

** Hue Distribution Examples

Everforest accents span H0-H340 (full wheel) BUT all at very low saturation,
so the variation is subtle. With high saturation, a full-wheel spread looks
chaotic.

Nord accents cluster around H0-H200 (warm reds to cool blues), skipping
the magenta/violet range almost entirely.

Gruvbox accents all shift warm — even the "blue" (#83a598) has a teal/green
tint, not a pure blue.

Ember's accents span ~90 degrees in the warm range, creating a cohesive
"embers and ash" palette where every color feels like it belongs to the
same fire.

* Applying These Rules: Ember's Design Decisions

** The "One Ember in the Ash" Principle

Most themes distribute saturation evenly across accents. Ember does the
opposite: eight accents at ultra-low saturation (S20-35%) plus one hero
(coral #e08060 at S55%) that cuts through like a match struck in the dark.

This gives Ember its distinctive character:
- Keywords glow in coral — bold, assertive, warm
- Functions shimmer in gold — the second brightest but still muted
- Strings sit in olive — natural, organic
- Everything else recedes into the warm graphite

** Three Variants, One Soul

| Variant      | Background | L%  | Description              |
|--------------+-----------+-----+--------------------------|
| *Ember*      | =#1c1b19= | 10% | Dark graphite — the default |
| *Ember Soft* | =#242320= | 13% | Lifted graphite, softer contrast |
| *Ember Light*| =#e6dac4= | 86% | Warm ivory, darkened accents |

The light variant doesn't just invert — it darkens all accents to maintain
the same contrast ratios and perceptual hierarchy. The coral hero becomes
a deeper terra cotta (#b84c30) that still leads the eye.

** Semantic Mapping: Coral Keywords

Ember maps coral to keywords — the most frequently occurring syntax element.
This makes coral the heartbeat of the theme: every =if=, =def=, =return=,
=import= pulses with the hero accent. Functions get gold (semibold), types
get gold (bold italic), and strings get olive.

The mapping is intentional: warm color on keywords feels bold and assertive;
cool color would feel technical and precise. Ember chose warmth.

* Designing New Themes: A Reproducible Process

If you want to create your own theme using these principles, here is a
7-step process.

** Step 1: Seed Palette

Start at [[https://coolors.co][coolors.co]] or similar tool. Generate or curate a 4-6 color palette
that *feels* right. Don't overthink it — this is raw inspiration.

What you're looking for:
- A color that could be the *hero accent* (the personality of the theme)
- A dark color that hints at the *background hue*
- A muted/neutral that could inform *foreground warmth*
- 1-2 colors that create *tension or harmony* with the hero

*Output:* 4-6 hex colors + their names/feelings.

** Step 2: Extract Design Parameters

Every theme is defined by a combination of parameters. The more parameters
you push to unusual values, the more distinctive (and potentially polarizing)
the result.

*** Identity Hue (from seed palette)

Pick the hue that will tint your background ramp.

| Hue Range | Character                           | Examples         |
|-----------+-------------------------------------+------------------|
| H0-30     | Warm brown/rust                     | Gruvbox          |
| H30-60    | Gold/amber                          | Ember            |
| H100-150  | Green/forest                        | Everforest       |
| H150-180  | Teal/cyan                           | —                |
| H190-210  | Ocean blue                          | Solarized        |
| H220-250  | Indigo/navy                         | Nord             |
| H250-280  | Purple/violet                       | Catppuccin       |

*** Temperature Strategy

| Strategy        | Description                           | Example           |
|-----------------+---------------------------------------+-------------------|
| All-warm        | Warm bg + warm accents                | Gruvbox, Ember    |
| All-cool        | Cool bg + cool accents                | Nord, Solarized   |
| Warm-on-cool    | Cool bg + warm hero accents (glow)    | —                 |
| Cool-on-warm    | Warm bg + cool hero accents (calm)    | rare              |

Warm-on-cool creates the most visual *tension* — warm accents "glow" against
cool backgrounds. Ember takes the all-warm approach for cohesion.

*** Saturation Strategy

| Strategy          | Accent S% | Feel                 | Example              |
|-------------------+-----------+----------------------+----------------------|
| Ultra-muted       | 15-25%    | Zen, minimal         | —                    |
| Muted/natural     | 28-42%    | Comfortable, earthy  | Everforest           |
| Moderate          | 38-55%    | Balanced, versatile  | —                    |
| Vivid-dark        | 55-75%    | Bold but controlled  | Solarized, Gruvbox   |
| Pastel            | 50-70%+   | Bright, playful      | Catppuccin           |
| Monochrome hero   | 20-35% + one at 55% | Focused, dramatic | Ember          |

*** CIELAB Lightness Target

| Target L*  | Feel                                | Contrast with dark bg |
|------------+-------------------------------------+-----------------------|
| L*40-48    | Dark, subdued, elegant              | Low — subtle tokens   |
| L*48-55    | Balanced, Solarized-like            | Medium — comfortable  |
| L*55-62    | Bright, clear, Everforest-like      | High — crisp tokens   |
| L*65-80    | Pastel, airy, Catppuccin-like       | Very high — floaty    |

*The band width matters as much as the target.* Keep all accents within 5-8
L* units of each other.

*** Hue Spread (accent diversity)

| Spread       | Degrees | Character                    | Example        |
|--------------+---------+------------------------------+----------------|
| Monochrome   | ~30 deg | Single-hue variations        | —              |
| Analogous    | ~90 deg | Adjacent hues, very cohesive | Ember          |
| Triadic      | ~180 deg| Three zones, balanced        | —              |
| Full wheel   | ~300+   | All hues present             | Nord, Catppuccin |

** Step 3: Build the Background Ramp

Take the identity hue and build 11 stops:

1. Start with bg at your identity hue, S10-12%, L8-10%
2. Step through to base8 at the same hue, S10-12%, L76-80%
3. Increase lightness by ~3-8% per step
4. Keep saturation flat (S8-15%) across the ramp
5. fg and fg-alt can break the hue rule for warmth

*NEVER use a seed palette color directly as bg.* Extract only the hue.

** Step 4: Adapt Seed Colors to Accent Slots

1. *Identify roles:* Which seed color maps to which accent slot?
2. *Normalize L*:* Convert to CIELAB and adjust until within your target band
3. *Adjust saturation:* Pull all accents into your saturation range
4. *Fill gaps:* Derive missing accents by shifting hue +/- 30 degrees
5. *Verify:* No two accents within 20 degrees hue AND similar L*

** Step 5: Choose Semantic Mappings

| Choice                | Feel                           |
|-----------------------+--------------------------------|
| Warm keywords         | Bold, assertive (Ember)        |
| Cool keywords         | Technical, precise             |
| Bright functions      | Energetic, sparky              |
| Muted functions       | Calm, professional             |

Two themes with identical hex palettes but different semantic mappings will
feel like completely different themes.

** Step 6: Write the Theme

Include ALL face overrides (~95+). The "minimal overrides" approach sounds
elegant but produces themes that look broken when users open magit, dired,
or completion popups and see colors from a different palette.

** Step 7: Iterate Visually

Load the theme and check:
1. *Code buffer* (elisp, JS, Python) — is the token hierarchy clear?
2. *Org file* — do headings, blocks, and TODO states look intentional?
3. *Org agenda* — are dates, scheduled items, and deadlines distinguishable?
4. *Magit* — do added/removed diffs use clear green/red?
5. *Mu4e* — are unread/flagged/read emails visually distinct?
6. *Completion popup* — does the current selection stand out?
7. *Minibuffer* — is the prompt visible and the match highlighting clear?

The squint test: unfocus your eyes and look at a code buffer. If one color
jumps out, it's too bright or too saturated. Adjust its L* or S% until the
buffer looks evenly lit.

* Color Utilities

Doom provides these transformation functions:

- =(doom-darken COLOR AMOUNT)= — darken by 0.0-1.0
- =(doom-lighten COLOR AMOUNT)= — lighten by 0.0-1.0
- =(doom-blend COLOR1 COLOR2 AMOUNT)= — blend two colors (0.0 = COLOR2, 1.0 = COLOR1)

Use these for derived colors to maintain consistency.
